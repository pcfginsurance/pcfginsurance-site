## Farm Property Insurance

### What It Covers

| Asset | Coverage |
|-------|----------|
| Farm buildings | Barns, silos, storage |
| Equipment | Tractors, combines, tools |
| Livestock | Dairy cows, cattle, horses |
| Feed/supplies | Stored hay, grain, supplies |

### Coverage Types

| Type | What It Pays |
|------|--------------|
| Replacement cost | New replacement |
| Actual cash value | Depreciated value |
| Agreed value | Pre-agreed amount |

### Important Considerations

- **Functional replacement** — Some policies pay for similar function, not exact replacement
- **Equipment floaters** — Portable equipment needs separate coverage
- **Livestock limits** — Per-animal and total limits

## FAQ: Farm Insurance in NY

**Q: Do I need farm insurance if I only have a hobby farm?**
A: If you have any income or employees, you likely need coverage. Homeowner's policy won't cover farm operations.

**Q: What does "functional replacement" mean?**
A: Some policies pay to replace with similar function, not exact style. Can reduce premiums but may not cover upgrades.

**Q: Is crop insurance worth it?**
A: If you're dependent on crops for income, yes. Weather events can destroy entire harvests.

**Q: Do I need separate coverage for my farm stand?**
A: Likely yes. Farm property may not cover retail operations. Liability is especially important.

**Q: Can I insure my farm animals?**
A: Yes, livestock coverage is available. High-value animals may need scheduled coverage.
