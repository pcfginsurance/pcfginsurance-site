## Do You Need Workers' Comp Insurance in NY?

**Short answer: Almost certainly yes.**

New York State requires virtually all employers to carry workers' compensation insurance. This includes:
- Businesses with at least one employee
- Part-time workers
- Family members employed by the business
- Sole proprietors (if they elect coverage)

There are very few exceptions, and the penalties for non-compliance are severe—potential fines of up to $200,000 and criminal charges in serious cases.

## What Workers' Comp Covers

Workers' compensation insurance provides:

| Coverage Type | What It Includes |
|---------------|------------------|
| **Medical Benefits** | All reasonable medical expenses related to a work injury |
| **Disability Benefits** | Payments if an employee cannot work due to an injury |
| **Death Benefits** | Benefits to dependents if a worker dies from a work-related injury |
| **Rehabilitation** | Vocational rehabilitation services when applicable |

**Key point:** Workers' comp is a "no-fault" system. Employees receive benefits regardless of who caused the injury—protecting both them and you from costly lawsuits.

*These are estimates. Your actual premium depends on your industry classification, payroll, claims history, and safety programs.*

### Factors That Affect Your Premium

1. **Industry classification code** — Construction pays more than office work
2. **Payroll size** — Premiums are calculated per $100K of payroll
3. **Claims history** — Businesses with prior claims pay more
4. **Safety programs** — NYSIF and private carriers offer discounts for safety training
5. **Experience modification rate (EMR)** — Your claims ratio compared to industry average

## How to Get Workers' Comp Insurance in Upstate NY

### Option 1: NYSIF (New York State Insurance Fund)

NYSIF is the state-sponsored workers' comp fund. Many small businesses use NYSIF because:
- Competitive rates
- No profit motive
- Available to businesses that private insurers might reject

**Contact NYSIF:**
- Online: nysif.com

## FAQ: Workers' Comp for Upstate NY Small Businesses

**Q: Can I self-insure workers' comp in NY?**
A: Yes, but only for large employers (typically $500K+ in annual payroll) and with state approval. Most small businesses should not self-insure.

**Q: Does workers' comp cover remote employees?**
A: Generally, yes—if the work is being performed for your business, even from home, it typically covered. However, the injury must arise out of and occur in the course of employment.

**Q: Can I require employees to pay for their own workers' comp?**
A: No. Employers are prohibited from requiring employees to contribute to workers' comp costs in New York.

**Q: What if my employee gets injured commuting to work?**
A: Generally, commuting injuries are NOT covered. However, "特殊的出勤情况" (special circumstances like employer-provided transportation or required travel) may be covered.

**Q: How do I report a workers' comp claim in NY?**
A: Notify your insurance carrier immediately. They will file C-2 form with the NY Workers' Compensation Board. Employees should also be informed of their rights.
